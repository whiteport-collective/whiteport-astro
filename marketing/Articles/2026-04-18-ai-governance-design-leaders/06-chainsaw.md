## LinkedIn draft

Just because you give each of your team members a chainsaw doesn't mean the trees fall in the right direction.

This is the most common mistake I see in AI rollouts.

Leadership procures the tools. Sends out the licenses. Books a kickoff training. Crosses their fingers.

Then waits for the agentic productivity miracle.

Instead: confused team members. Trees falling sideways. Some on cars. Eventually, someone gets hurt — and the project goes on indefinite pause while legal investigates.

The chainsaw isn't the problem. The chainsaw is incredible.

What's missing is the **felling plan**. Where do we cut? In what order? Who shouts "timber"? Who's responsible if a tree lands on a building?

In AI terms, that felling plan is your **governance framework**:

🪵 What can each person decide alone?
🪵 What needs the team's call?
🪵 What gets escalated?
🪵 What happens if someone makes a wrong cut?

Without those four answers written down somewhere management has signed, your team won't pull the trigger on the chainsaw. They'll just look at it.

Got the chainsaws. Now write the felling plan.
