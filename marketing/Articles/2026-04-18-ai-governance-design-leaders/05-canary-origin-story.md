## LinkedIn draft

Consultants are the canaries in the coal mine.

When the market shifts, we're the first to stop getting hired. That's how I felt the AI shift before most of my friends did.

Here's the part I rarely say out loud:

For 8+ months I had no proper assignments. I was borrowing money from my mom to feed my son. I was building infrastructure (WDS, BMAD, Agent Space) full-time, with no certainty it would matter to anyone. The constant background hum of *"I have to do this, I don't know how, and I have nobody on my side."*

I tried to get my own dev team interested in AI early. They weren't. As one of them told me later: "We're too busy pouring water out of the sinking ship with buckets — we don't have time to learn how to swim."

That's not laziness. That's exactly the organizational problem I see in every brand I now work with.

The people who are good at yesterday's tools will never be the ones who push the envelope. That's why this can't come from the floor. It has to come from leadership — with a framework that makes change *safe* to attempt.

I went through 8 months of this so the next design leader doesn't have to.

If your team is stuck — DM me.
