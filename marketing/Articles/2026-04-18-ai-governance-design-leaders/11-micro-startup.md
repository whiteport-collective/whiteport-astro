## LinkedIn draft

What does an AI-enabled team actually do on Monday morning?

Forget "AI strategy workshops." Forget "innovation hackathons." Here's the operational pattern that works:

**Run each team like a micro-startup against the existing solution.**

The setup:

🎯 **A real business problem.** Either assigned from above or surfaced by the team itself. Describe the pain. Describe the consequences of *not* solving it. No vague "improve the experience."

⏱ **An insanely short deadline.** Days, not months. The pressure forces focus. Eliminates scope creep. Reveals what actually matters.

🧰 **Tightly defined resources.** Not "use whatever AI you want." Specific tools, specific data, specific constraints. Constraints are what make creativity sharp.

📊 **Measurable outcome.** Not "qualitative feedback." Numbers. Compared against the current solution. A/B testing against the status quo.

🤝 **Full ownership.** The team owns the problem end-to-end. They make decisions without waiting for permission. They shoulder the responsibility for the result.

This is what "intrapreneurship" actually looks like in practice. Not a culture deck. A weekly cycle.

But — here's the catch — this only works if the governance framework is already in place. Without it, "decide without permission" is reckless. With it, it's exactly what the company wanted.

Governance first. Micro-startups second.
