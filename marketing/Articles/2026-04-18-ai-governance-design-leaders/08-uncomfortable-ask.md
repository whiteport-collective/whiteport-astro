## LinkedIn draft

Asking a designer to drop Figma isn't a tool change. It's an identity shift.

I see design leaders make this ask casually. "Just try designing in code." "Let the AI handle the visual layer." Like it's about a software preference.

It isn't.

Figma has been the centerpiece of a designer's craft for years. Every shortcut is muscle memory. Every component library is hard-earned. Every pixel-perfect frame is part of how they prove their value to the org. To drop that — even partly — is to question who they *are* in the team.

So when "use AI now" comes from leadership without scaffolding, it lands like an arbitrary demand. The designer hears: *your craft doesn't matter anymore.*

That's why most rollouts stall. Not because of resistance to technology. Because of resistance to identity loss.

What governance gives you is a way to make this ask without making it personal:

🔹 Formal company decision (not the boss's whim)
🔹 Clear *why* (the policies explain the strategic reason)
🔹 Support structure (training, pair sessions, safe-to-fail experiments)
🔹 Realistic timeline (OKRs, not "by Friday")

Now the designer can move toward the new tools because the *organization* asked, with proper backing — not because they were told to abandon their craft overnight.

If you're about to make the ask, write the framework first.
