## LinkedIn draft

Here's the simplest AI governance rule I know.

*You are allowed to do whatever you want. As long as you produce this document, and clear it with a colleague who cosigns it with their name.*

That's it.

No central approval committee. No two-week review cycle. No legal-team-bottleneck.

Just two human names on a piece of work.

Why this works:

When *your* name is on it, you review properly. Not skim-and-approve. Properly. You ask the questions you'd want asked of your own work. You point out the gap that everyone else might miss.

The methodology isn't there to enable production. It's there to *change how you see the problem*. The act of cosigning forces both people to look at the work as if they own it — because, formally, they now do.

This solves three things at once:

🪶 **Speed** — no central bottleneck, decisions stay at the team level
🪶 **Quality** — two pairs of eyes, both with skin in the game
🪶 **Knowledge transfer** — the cosigner now knows what's in the work and why

Bonus: the more sponsors you can get to cosign, the more likely you get funding to push the work further. Cosigning also signals strategic alignment.
