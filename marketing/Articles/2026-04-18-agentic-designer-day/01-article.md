## LinkedIn (Mårten personlig)

This is my office now.

A forest path, a dog, and a head full of agent outputs to process.

Before AI, my days looked nothing like this. As a UX designer, I spent more time in my email client and calendar than in all my design tools combined. I did research. I sat in meetings with stakeholders trying to understand the world they operated in.

My understanding was distilled into a few sketches. Wireframes. Paper prototypes drawn by hand.

Then came the real work: communicating those screens to clients, colleagues, and developers. Gathering feedback. Redrawing. Going for another round. And when everything was finally approved, I'd explain it all over again to the development team — and iterate once more when something was lost in translation.

Short bursts of creativity, surrounded by endless ambassadorship for the users and the concept.

Then AI flipped a switch.

The seemingly endless meetings just... stopped. A brief conversation about the goal, the business requirements, and the constraints — that's everything needed now. With that basic input, I send my agents off to do what used to take hours of back-and-forth conversations.

I still direct. I still give feedback. I still provide the intent. But now it's agents doing the heavy lifting. My strength in meetings has been exchanged for hours in front of multiple agents, running in parallel.

And a strange new reality has emerged: when I'm not running, the agents stop.

A funny new daily routine has taken shape. I run the agents until my five-hour quota is filled — which usually happens within about three hours of actual work. Then I take a break. Go for a walk. Handle a few non-AI tasks that still remain.

The scope of what I can do has expanded in ways I didn't expect. I weigh in on architecture. I've reinforced my strategic thinking. I've learned to write code with the help of agents.

As a designer, I can now do something that used to take an entire team: I design, I specify, I develop, I review — and then I start on version two. And version three.

How did your day shift with AI? Do you spend much more time in front of the computer? Do you take breaks when you hit the subscription limits?

I'd love to hear your story.
