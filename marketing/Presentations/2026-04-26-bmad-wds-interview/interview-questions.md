# WDS × BMad — Interview Questions
Brian's cue questions for the Mårten interview.

---

## Intro

- You started in UX in 1999. Take us back.
- 26 years. Founded Whiteport 2003. What kind of work?
- Then AI came along. What changed for you?

---

## BMad & Getting Started

- How did you first come across BMad?
- You're a designer — what made you go deep into a developer tool?
- When did you realise BMad was the right home for WDS?
- What does WDS actually add to the BMad workflow?
- And now other people are using it — like me. How did that happen?

---

## The Core Thesis

- You say almost everyone does UX backwards. What does backwards look like?
- Walk me through what a typical process looks like today.
- Sound familiar to anyone watching?
- So what's the right order?

---

## Phase 1 — The Product Brief

- Clarity first. What does that mean in practice?
- How does AI change this phase?
- There's a practical output here that I don't think people expect.

---

## Phase 2 — The Trigger Map

- Phase two. You've got your brief. What's next?
- What is psychology in this context?
- And the AI runs this as a conversation?
- This is the part that most designers skip entirely.

---

## Phase 3 — Scenarios

- Clarity, psychology — now interaction. Scenarios.
- What is selective ignorance?
- And then the agent wireframes it?
- How does the wireframe become a real design?

---

## Phase 4 — Visual + Code

- Now we're in design and code. What does this look like?
- The backend has been running in parallel this whole time?
- And the test feeds back into the scenario?

---

## Close

- For the designer watching — what's the one shift?
- WDS. Whiteport Design Studio method.
