## LinkedIn (Mårten personlig)

I sat down with Brian from the BMad community to talk about something that bothers me every time I see a design review.

Most designers do UX backwards.

They open Figma first. They sketch, wireframe, prototype — and somewhere in the middle they try to figure out what they're actually building. Backwards from pixels to purpose.

Brian came in from a different angle. He's not a developer. Six months ago he didn't know what Git was. But he found the WDS method, got hooked on the thinking behind it, and ended up writing his own module for it.

In this conversation we cover:
→ Why starting with the interface is the most expensive mistake in design
→ What Trigger Mapping actually is (it's not personas)
→ The principle of selective ignorance — and why radical focus beats feature completeness every time
→ How the whole method collapses the strategy–design–code cycle

If you're a designer wondering where AI actually fits into your process — this is the conversation.

## Bio — Mårten Angner

> *For YouTube description, show notes, or any written context.*

Mårten Angner is a UX designer and digital product manager based in Stockholm, Sweden. He founded Whiteport in 2003 and has spent over two decades building digital products — websites, apps, and e-commerce solutions — for clients across industries.

He is the creator of WDS (Whiteport Design Studio method), an AI-assisted design workflow built on top of Claude Code. WDS guides teams through product strategy, user psychology, UX, and implementation in a structured sequence — using specialist AI agents at each phase.
