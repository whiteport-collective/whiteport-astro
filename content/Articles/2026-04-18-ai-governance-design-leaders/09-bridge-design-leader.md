## LinkedIn draft

Three layers of reality in your organization right now.

**IT and management** can turn on the language model. They have the budget, the authority, the procurement power. But they don't know what your team needs. They don't know what the risks look like in practice. So how can they give you the formal go-ahead for something they can't fully see?

**Your individual team members** can be courageous and start deploying AI all day. They have the curiosity and the skill. But if they're responsible — and most people in organizations are — they won't take initiative without a formal mandate, proper guard rails, and clarity on what's expected of them.

**That leaves you.**

You sit in the middle of the chain. You're the only one who can:

→ Listen to the concerns of each designer on the floor
→ Still get an audience in the management team
→ Actually have the equipment to come up with a solution

You feel the pain of the entire chain. Management feels their layer. Team members feel theirs. Only you feel both.

That's why this can't be delegated up or down. It can't wait for IT's roadmap. It can't wait for the team to "self-organize."

Nobody else in the organization will initiate the AI governance and compliance initiative.

It has to be you.

What's your first move?
