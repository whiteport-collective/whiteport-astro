# 13 — The New Job (Training Unlock)

## LinkedIn draft

Just because you give everyone in the design team chainsaws doesn't mean the trees will start falling.

AI is a sharp tool. You can give every designer access to agents — but that isn't enough. Nobody wants to be the first one to find out what happens when their deployment crashes the system.

Here's what most leaders miss:

Designing and developing with AI isn't a new *skill* at work.

It's a new *job*.

Something completely different.

Yes — domain knowledge still matters. It always will. But that leverage is dead weight until your designers have actually learned the new tools, in their hands, in real conditions.

We have to allow time for basic training. Real time. Not a webinar. Not a Friday afternoon. Time.

Because here's what happens then: the lights come on inside their heads. They build a relationship with the agents. They start to *think* agentically.

And once that happens — you can't stop them.

---

If you're rolling out AI without protected training time, you're handing out chainsaws and hoping for the best.

Block the calendar. Make it a job, not a side quest. Watch what happens.
