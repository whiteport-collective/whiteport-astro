## The hook

Most design leaders are stuck in the gap between AI enthusiasm and AI execution. The blocker isn't tools or training — it's the absence of an organizational mandate.

Here's the 2026 checklist that closes that gap.

---

## The Checklist

### ☐ 1. Write a vision for AI Adoption

A one-page statement: *what AI changes for our users, our team, our craft.* Without this, every initiative is justified case-by-case and dies in committee. The vision is what gives every downstream decision its "why".

### ☐ 2. Put AI Governance & Compliance framework in place

Three layers: **compliance** (how we adhere, what happens at breach), **autonomy** (what each role can decide alone, with their team, what gets escalated), **policies** (GDPR, WCAG, incident handling, down to the last team member). Without it, responsible people freeze. With it, they ship.

### ☐ 3. Choose an Agentic design framework

Pick one. Stop evaluating. Bmad. WDS. Whatever fits — but make a formal choice so your team stops wasting cycles comparing methodologies and starts compounding inside one.

### ☐ 4. Allow time for basic training

Block calendar time. AI literacy is a skill, not a memo. The team that practices in low-stakes settings becomes the team that performs in high-stakes ones.

### ☐ 5. Initiate an Intrapreneurship culture

Give people the formal mandate to identify, prototype, and ship AI workflows from inside the org. Without intrapreneurship, AI adoption stays a leadership monologue. With it, every team member becomes a force multiplier.

### ☐ 6. Celebrate small victories

The agentic shift won't arrive as one big launch — it arrives as a hundred small wins compounding. Make those wins visible, name them, retell them. What gets celebrated gets repeated.
