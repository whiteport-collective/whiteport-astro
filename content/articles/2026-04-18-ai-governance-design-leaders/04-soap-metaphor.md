## LinkedIn draft

AI in your organization is the soap in the shower.

Everyone knows you need it. Everyone agrees it matters. And it's *impossibly* hard to pick up.

Here's why.

The people with the formal mandate (management) don't know what's needed at the team level. But they will bear *all* the consequences when something goes wrong. A GDPR breach. A hallucination in production. A reputational hit.

So they sit on the bar of soap. They wait. They ask vague questions about "productivity gains."

Meanwhile your designers and developers can see exactly what's possible — but they have no formal go-ahead, no guard rails, no clarity on what's expected. So they wait too.

Three layers, all waiting on each other:
- Management: "Tell me what you need."
- Team: "Tell me what's allowed."
- The soap: still on the floor.

Someone has to bend down and pick it up. And that someone — uncomfortable as it sounds — is the design leader. You're the only one who feels the pain of the entire chain.

Who in your org is reaching for the soap?
