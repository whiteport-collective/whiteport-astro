# Social posts — Figma first is killing your agentic design workflow

## LinkedIn

Most designers open Figma before they understand what they're building.

That was fine when developers interpreted designs manually. It's a problem when agents build from them.

Here's what happens: agents don't read Figma files the way developers do. They need dense context — purpose, behavior, content, error states. Figma gives them pixels.

Pixels without context = vibe coding. An endless loop of prompts that makes your project worse with each iteration.

The fix isn't AI drawing for you in Figma. That just gets you lost faster.

The shift that actually works:
→ Strategy first — what are you building and why?
→ Scenarios — what does it actually do, step by step?
→ Agentic implementation — let agents build from real specs
→ Figma last — open the code for visual refinement

A complete system for designers ready to work with agents across their entire design process. Strategy, scenarios, agentic development — with a full curriculum to get you there.

The designers who make this shift don't just work faster.
They think more clearly. They write better briefs. They build things that actually work.
