# Will Designers Go Down Figma-in-Hand?

**The designer identity crisis nobody is talking about.**

---

**Image:** Stormy ocean. A fist clenching desperately, fingers curling around the Figma logo as it goes under. Dramatic, painterly. The grip is fierce but the outcome is clear.

---

For the past decade, being a designer has meant one thing above all: being a Figma wizard. Mastery of the tool became synonymous with mastery of the craft. Job postings required it. Portfolio reviews were often really Figma file reviews. The tool and the professional merged into one.

We designers were the gatekeepers of design. No one got anything out without it landing on the designers desk. With the position came great powers. We designers were important. Everyone adapted to us and our needs. 

*Then AI agents arrived!*

And suddenly, anyone could make decent looking design with a single prompt. The tool that defined us is no longer the bottleneck. If someone wanted design they simply circled us. And went ahead without us. 

The demand for pixel pushers plummeted. The silence was deafening!

Designers everywhere are currently in full coping mode and very few seems to get the message. 

*Figma is a tool. It was never the job!*

Designers who built their entire professional identity around Figma proficiency are in genuine trouble — not because Figma is going away, but because Figma proficiency is no longer a defensible moat.

An agent can generate a Figma file. It can fill it with components, apply styles, simulate layouts. The visual output — the thing that once required years of practice — is now produceable in minutes by anyone who can describe what they want clearly.

*Still designers hold on to this single tool like their life depends on it*

Drawing interfaces, hand crafting visual design is off course still an important part of our jobs as desiners. But, the bottleneck has shifted. The Design role that is desired today is much wider and actually offer somethign the old model never offereded. A seat at the table. 

What agents cannot do is set direction, define intent and make strategic decisins. We desiner happen to be perfectly suited for being the ones prividing the nececary direction to agents. 

We designers are well trained to hold the product vision in mind, through hundreds of pages and hold the product vision alive and aligned while balancing user needs, technical constraints, business realities, and brand truth. They cannot push back when a stakeholder asks for something that will confuse users. They cannot feel the friction in a flow.

That is the designer's job. It always was. Figma was just the delivery mechanism.

*Clinging to the wrong end of the stick!*

Some designers respond to this shift by doubling down on Figma mastery. Learning the latest auto-layout tricks. Getting excited about Figma AI features. Looking for the next plugin. Or, having Agents draw directly in Figma. This is clinging with both hands to a fist closing around a sinking log. To scared to realize the new sheriff in town

**With AI, Design must live in code!**

The designers who will thrive in this new landscape are not the ones who can use Figma the fastest. They are the ones who can think the clearest. Who can turn messy user research into structured strategic documents. Who can guide an AI agent from insight through specification to working code without losing the thread of why it matters to a real person with a real problem. And, Use Figma and other deisgn tools as mechnisms of agent training. 

*The Designsers role does not disappear. It expands!*

This is exactly what WDS, Whiteport Desgins Studio is doing for any agentic designer on the planet. This official UX plugin for BMad methiod, is helping you as a designers to become a person who who now commands a team of infinitely patient, tireless agents who can execute at machine speed — but only if the brief is clear enough.

The designer becomes the brain. The agents become the hands.

Figma still has a role. But in the WDS process it is the last step, not the first. WDS lets your design system do the heavy lifing and we open any page that is not holding up to our standards in Figma to adjust the design and immediately save the design back into desing systems and code to make the agents even better then next time around.
Where you make the final visual decisions from a position of knowledge rather than hope.

*Figma last. Intention and clarity first.*

Designers who make this shift will not just survive the agentic transition. They will define it.

The ones clinging to the tool will not go quietly. But they will go.
