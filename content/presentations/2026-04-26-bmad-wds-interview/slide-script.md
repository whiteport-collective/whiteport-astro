## SLIDE 02 — The Spec is the New Code

**Type:** Statement slide

**Label (mono):** THE SHIFT

**Headline (large, gradient):**
> Specifications is the new code.

**Two-part build:**

**Part 1 — The Specification is the new code:**
For AI to work autonomously, you need to tell it what to do. Precisely. That's what specifications are — the instruction set that replaces prompting.

**Part 2 — Agentic design is done in code:**
Don't use Figma to design the software. Use Figma to teach agents how to design it autonomously.

**Visual:** Two distinct blocks animate in sequence. The contrast between "old role" and "new role" is the point.

**Purpose:** Defines what agentic design actually means. The designer's job shifts from executing to teaching.

## SLIDE 04 — The WDS Sequence

**Label (mono):** THE WDS METHOD

```
                   📄  PRODUCT BRIEF
                            │
         ┌──────────────────┴──────────────────┐
         │                                      │
         ▼                                      ▼
⚙️  PLATFORM BUILD                      👥  TRIGGER MAP

                                        🖼  VISUAL DESIGN
         │                                      │

                            ▼

                    (UX layered onto platform)
```

**Purpose:** Shows the actual WDS process — strategy is shared foundation, then platform and UX are built simultaneously and joined at the end.

## SLIDE 05 — Phase 01: Product Brief

**Type:** Phase section slide

**Phase pill:** 01 PRODUCT BRIEF

**Headline:**
> Your project's constitution.

**Five questions (stacked, large labels):**
1. What are we building and why does it matter?
2. Who is this for?
3. How will we know it's successful?
4. What else exists and how are we different?
5. What are our boundaries?

**Key insight (accent color, bottom):**
> When everyone can answer these questions the same way, you have alignment. When they can't, you have chaos.

**Purpose:** What Phase 1 produces and why it matters before anything else.

---

## SLIDE 06 — Phase 02: Trigger Map

**Type:** Statement + diagram slide

**Phase pill:** 02 TRIGGER MAP

**Headline:**
> A system needs to be used — and used in the intended way — for business value to occur.

**Sequential build (each step animates in):**

```
STEP 1 → BUSINESS GOALS
         What does the business need to achieve?

STEP 2 → THE PRODUCT
         The system that creates the value

STEP 3 → THE USER
         Who needs to use it — and in what way?

STEP 4 → DRIVING FORCES
         What drives them toward it?
         What holds them back?
```

**Caption:**
Business value only occurs when the system is used — and used in the intended way. The Trigger Map maps that connection.

**Purpose:** Builds the logic step by step. Each layer earns its place before the next appears.

---

## SLIDE 08 — Phase 03: Scenarios

**Type:** Phase section slide

**Phase pill:** 03 SCENARIOS

**Headline:**
> Journeys, not pages.

**Key statement:**
In WDS, the pages ARE the flowchart. No separate abstract diagram. The scenario is a visual arrangement of actual screen sketches showing the journey from start to desired state.

**Three elements (cards):**
- **THE SCENARIO** — Linear sunshine path. Screen sketches arranged in flow.
- **THE STORYBOARD** — State variations within each screen.
- **THE SPECIFICATION** — Edge cases and business rules. In text, separately.

**Purpose:** What a scenario is, and why it's different from designing pages in isolation.

---

## SLIDE 09 — Big Quote

**Type:** Full-screen typographic quote with brief explanation

**Quote (large, warm gradient):**
> "Selective ignorance."

**Explanation (medium, below):**
Focus on what gives the best experience and value to the business for the vast majority of the most valuable customers in the most valuable transactions. Design the sunshine path first. Edge cases come later — separately, so the scenario stays clean.

**Small line at bottom:**
> Selective ignorance is not laziness. It's strategic focus.

**Purpose:** A memorable concept that reframes how to approach complexity.

---

## SLIDE 10 — The Paradigm Shift

**Type:** Statement slide — the synthesis

**Label (mono):** THE SHIFT

**Three-line sequence (large, stacked):**
```
The design becomes the specification.
The specification becomes the product.
The code is just the printout.
```

**Body (below):**
Your design thinking no longer stops at handoff. It scales infinitely. Every specification you write becomes a permanent record of your design reasoning — for developers, stakeholders, and AI alike. No more endless prompting sessions.

**Purpose:** The "aha" moment. The new role of the designer in the AI era.
