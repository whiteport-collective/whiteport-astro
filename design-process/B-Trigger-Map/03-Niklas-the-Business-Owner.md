# Niklas the Business Owner — Secondary Persona

> Secondary target — finds Whiteport through content or referral, books a meeting, becomes a client

**Priority:** SECONDARY
**Role in Flywheel:** Discovers Whiteport → books meeting → hires for project → gets results → refers others
**Created:** 2026-03-04

---

## Profile Summary

**Niklas is the SMB owner who knows his website needs to be better but doesn't know where to start.** He's running a business — maybe a construction firm, a restaurant group, or a niche e-commerce brand in southern Sweden. He's heard "AI can help" from every direction but it all sounds the same. He needs someone who speaks business results, not tech jargon.

Niklas is the revenue engine. Every Niklas who books a meeting and signs a project directly drives Whiteport's primary business goal.

---

## Background

### Business Journey

**Company:** 10-50 employees, established 5-15 years, solid revenue but limited digital presence
**Role:** Founder/owner or managing director — makes the final call on investments
**Technical Background:** Uses iPhone, email, maybe Instagram for business. Has heard of "ChatGPT" but hasn't used it strategically. Has a WordPress site that "someone built 3 years ago"
**Management Style:** Hands-on, values relationships, prefers talking to a person over reading documentation

---

## Current Situation

### Business Context

**The Digital Gap:**
- Website was built years ago, looks dated, doesn't reflect current quality of the business
- Competitors have better online presence — Niklas notices when clients mention it
- Tried hiring a "web guy" before — expensive, slow, result was just okay
- Knows social media matters but posting feels like shouting into the void

**The AI Confusion:**
- Everyone says "AI this, AI that" but Niklas can't tell what's real
- His nephew told him about ChatGPT but he doesn't see how it helps his business
- Worried about being sold expensive tech he doesn't need
- Wants results, not experiments

---

## Psychological Profile

### Personality & Motivations

**Core Identity:** Practical builder. Built his business through hard work, good relationships, and common sense. Respects expertise but distrusts consultants who use too many buzzwords.

**Decision Style:** Relationship-first. Needs to trust the person before trusting the solution. Will invest significantly if convinced — but needs proof, not promises. Often decides after a single good meeting where he "gets a feeling" for the person.

**Communication Preference:** Face-to-face or phone call. Short emails. Doesn't read long documents. Needs someone to explain things in plain Swedish (or English).

---

## Driving Forces

### Top 3 Positive Drivers (What Niklas Wants)

**1. Look professional online — have a website that matches the quality of his actual business**
- Niklas is proud of what he's built. The website doesn't reflect it
- When clients visit the site, Niklas wants them to think "this is a serious operation"
- Success looks like: a client saying "I checked your website — looks great"
- **Whiteport Promise:** A modern, fast, beautifully designed site that works as a digital business card

**2. Get a partner, not a vendor — someone who understands his business and handles the tech**
- Niklas doesn't want to "manage a website." He wants someone who just makes it work
- He values long-term relationships — wants to call one person who knows his situation
- Success looks like: "I call Mårten, tell him what I need, and it happens"
- **Whiteport Promise:** The AI agent workflow means Niklas can literally tell ChatGPT what to update and it happens through Git — or call Mårten and he handles it

**3. Stay competitive without becoming a tech company**
- Niklas sees competitors with slick websites, active social media, good Google rankings
- He doesn't want to hire a marketing team — he wants smart solutions that work with minimal effort
- Success looks like: showing up on Google, looking modern, without changing how he runs his business
- **Whiteport Promise:** The static Astro site is fast, SEO-friendly, and costs nearly nothing to host

---

### Top 3 Negative Drivers (What Niklas Fears)

**1. Fear of being sold expensive tech he doesn't need — another consultant who overcomplicates things**
- Niklas has been burned before. "Digital transformation" projects that delivered PowerPoints, not results
- Hearing "AI" makes him suspicious — sounds like a reason to charge more
- Failure looks like: spending 200,000 SEK on a website project that doesn't bring in a single new client
- **Whiteport Answer:** The site is the demo. Fast, static, nearly free to host. No ongoing platform fees. No mystery

**2. Fear of losing control — what if the tech makes him dependent on someone he can't understand?**
- Niklas has seen businesses locked into platforms they can't escape (expensive WP hosting, proprietary CMS)
- Doesn't want to be helpless if Whiteport isn't available
- Failure looks like: "I can't even update my own phone number on the website"
- **Whiteport Answer:** Everything is in Git (plain text files). Niklas owns the code. Even ChatGPT can edit it

**3. Frustrated by slow results — wants to see something working, not hear about "phases" and "sprints"**
- Niklas runs a business. He doesn't have patience for 6-month timelines
- When he decides to invest, he wants visible progress quickly
- Failure looks like: three meetings about "strategy" and still no website
- **Whiteport Answer:** Static Astro site can go from scaffold to live in days, not months. Content goes live in 30 seconds

---

## Validation Strategy

### What Niklas Needs to See About Whiteport

**1. Proof of Results**
- Case studies or portfolio showing real businesses (not just design showcases)
- Before/after — what did the old site look like? What does the new one do?
- "This client got X more inquiries after the rebuild"

**2. Mårten as a Person**
- Photo, background, personality
- Niklas wants to hire a person, not a company
- The about page needs to feel human and competent

**3. Clear, Simple Services**
- "We rebuild your website with AI" — not "We implement agent-driven content architectures"
- Price range or "starting from" indicator — Niklas needs to know if this is in his budget before calling
- "Book a free 30-minute chat" — low commitment first step

**4. Speed Signals**
- "Your site can be live in X weeks"
- "Content updates in 30 seconds"
- "No monthly platform fees"

## Conversion Path

### How Niklas Validates Whiteport

**Phase 1: Discovery**
- Google search, LinkedIn post from Alex-type, or word of mouth from Lisa-type
- Lands on whiteport.com, scans for 30 seconds

**Phase 2: Evaluation**
- Checks: does this look professional? Is there a real person behind it? Do they do what I need?
- Looks at portfolio/stream for relevant work
- Reads the services page — is this for businesses like mine?

**Phase 3: Contact**
- Books a meeting or calls directly
- The meeting is the real conversion — website just gets him there

**Phase 4: Advocacy**
- After project delivery, tells business network
- "My web guy uses AI, you should talk to him"
