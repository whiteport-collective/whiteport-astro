# Lisa the Loyal Client — Tertiary Persona

> Tertiary target — existing Whiteport client who manages content and refers others

**Priority:** TERTIARY
**Role in Flywheel:** Receives great delivery → manages content via ChatGPT → validates choice → refers other business owners
**Created:** 2026-03-04

---

## Profile Summary

**Lisa is the existing Whiteport client who already trusts Mårten and has a delivered Astro site.** She runs a boutique business — maybe a design studio, a wellness brand, or a specialty retailer. She's not technical but she's capable and curious. The AI content workflow makes her feel empowered rather than dependent.

Lisa is the proof that the model works. When she updates her own site through ChatGPT and tells her business friends about it, that's the most powerful marketing Whiteport can have.

---

## Background

### Business Context

**Company:** Micro-business, 1-5 employees, creative or service-oriented
**Relationship with Whiteport:** 6+ months, had her site built by Mårten, satisfied with the result
**Tech Comfort:** Uses ChatGPT for various tasks, comfortable with simple workflows, doesn't want to learn coding
**Content Needs:** Updates her blog/stream 1-2x per week, adds new portfolio items monthly, occasional page edits

---

## Current Situation

### The Content Reality

- Loves her site — it's fast, looks great, clients compliment it
- Updates content by telling ChatGPT "write me a blog post about X" and then pushing to GitHub (or asking Mårten)
- Sometimes unsure if she's doing it right — "did the post actually go live?"
- Wants to do more with the site but doesn't want to bother Mårten for every small thing

---

## Psychological Profile

**Core Identity:** Independent, creative, values aesthetics and quality. Proud of having a "modern" website. Enjoys telling people "my site is AI-powered."

**Relationship to Tech:** Not afraid of it, but needs clear instructions. Will follow a workflow reliably once she understands it. Gets frustrated by error messages or unclear feedback.

**Communication Style:** Prefers voice messages or short texts. Appreciates when things "just work."

---

## Driving Forces

### Top 3 Positive Drivers

**1. Feel independent — update her own site without needing a developer every time**
- Lisa doesn't want to wait 3 days for a blog post to go live
- Wants to feel capable: "I can do this myself"
- **Whiteport Promise:** ChatGPT + GitHub workflow gives her real autonomy

**2. Look cutting-edge to her own clients — "my website uses AI"**
- Lisa's clients are impressed when she mentions the AI workflow
- It positions her as modern and forward-thinking in her own industry
- **Whiteport Promise:** The workflow is genuinely impressive and easy to explain

**3. Feel taken care of — know that Mårten is there if something goes wrong**
- Independence doesn't mean abandonment. Lisa wants a safety net
- When something breaks or she needs a bigger change, she wants one call to fix it
- **Whiteport Promise:** Whiteport as ongoing partner, not just project delivery

---

### Top 3 Negative Drivers

**1. Fear of breaking her site — accidentally publishing something wrong or deleting content**
- Git feels powerful but also intimidating — "what if I push the wrong thing?"
- **Whiteport Answer:** Draft/locked content, build validation, easy rollback via Git

**2. Fear of looking incompetent — asking "dumb questions" about how to update content**
- Lisa doesn't want to feel like a burden
- **Whiteport Answer:** Clear documentation, content templates, ChatGPT-friendly schemas

**3. Frustrated by invisible results — "did it work? Is it live? Can people see it?"**
- After pushing content, Lisa wants confirmation
- **Whiteport Answer:** Build notifications, deploy previews, staging URLs

---

## How Lisa Discovers Ongoing Value

### The Recognition Path

**Phase 1: Experience the Difference**
- Updates a blog post through ChatGPT in 5 minutes instead of emailing a developer and waiting
- Realizes she published 3 posts this month vs. 0 last quarter with WordPress

**Phase 2: Recognition**
- "This workflow actually changed how I think about my online presence"
- Posts more frequently because the friction is gone

**Phase 3: Appreciation**
- Credits Mårten and Whiteport in conversations
- Feels loyalty — wouldn't switch to another developer

**Phase 4: Word of Mouth**
- Business friend complains about their website → Lisa says "you should talk to my guy"
- Shares the experience naturally, not as a sales pitch

## Impact on Business Goals

**Goal 1 (Client Acquisition):** Lisa's referrals are the highest-converting leads — warm introductions from a satisfied client
**Goal 2 (Thought Leadership):** Lisa is living proof that the methodology works for non-technical business owners
**Goal 3 (Productized Offering):** Lisa validates that the ChatGPT + GitHub workflow is genuinely usable by real clients
