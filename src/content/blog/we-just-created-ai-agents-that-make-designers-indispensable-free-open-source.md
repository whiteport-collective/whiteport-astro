The design market is changing fast. Assignments are fewer. AI is moving in. And many of us quietly wonder: *Who needs a designer?*

The truth is that the world needs designers more than ever.

It takes a specific type of brain to balance **complex information about a project and create a simple, useful interface.** That’s not something AI, entrepreneurs, or developers can do by themselves.

The challenge is that today’s world asks designers to step up and deliver more than visuals.

That’s why we built the **Whiteport Sketch to Code Expansion (WPS2C)** for the BMad method. It’s a new way for designers to **move the design process into the development environment (IDE)** and deliver **complete product documentation** as ready-to-use artifacts side by side with the codebase. Your output becomes the perfect base for humans and AI agents to build code from directly.

With **WPS2C and its AI Agents**, you’ll be able to deliver:

- **Target group analysis, personas, and strategic documentation** - Understanding the value of the product

- **Sketches, concepts, specifications -** Designs described in detail, perfect for both developers and AI to build from.

- **Content foundations** in multiple languages, with error texts and instructions delivered alongside your design.

- **Design systems** that guarantee consistency across the entire product.

- **Wireframes in code** using real components that developers can plug directly into the codebase.

By teaming up with AI Agents, you evolve from being&nbsp;*“the one drawing pretty pictures in the corner”* to becoming the person **at the center of attention — where the magic actually happens.**

In the new world of AI code generation, it’s not raw visuals that matter most.
👉 It’s the **concepts and specifications** that drive everything. And that’s exactly where designers can now shine.

The world doesn’t just need designers.
✨ The world needs **designers who step forward, expand their craft, and show how indispensable they really are — now and in the future. This is the future of design, and it starts now.**
