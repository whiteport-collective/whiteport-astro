Do you know how to sketch? I believe you can!

Look at this sketch from an actual project meeting. Simple circles, rough lines, basic shapes. Seemingly totally incomprehensable.

But, since it was sketched during the conversation, everyone in the room immediately understood what each part meant. When we needed to reference an idea, we just pointed to the sketch. When someone joined late, we could explain our entire discussion by walking through the drawing.

This isn't about being an artist. It's about communication.

We think visually. When you put pen to paper, you're not just drawing - you're externalizing your thoughts in a way others can see and build upon. The same principle works with AI. Instead of struggling with text descriptions that never quite capture what you want, a simple sketch often communicates your vision perfectly.

Could you have drawn this? Of course. These are just basic shapes and lines.

That's the point.

When talking to an AI agent, things are exactly the same. Show, dont tell! If you upload a sketch you can write far far less. Sometimes AI is telling you things about your sketch you did not even realized.
